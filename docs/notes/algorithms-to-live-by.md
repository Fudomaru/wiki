# Algorithms to Live By
### *A Digital Dojo Field Guide*

Welcome, wanderer. 
This dojo is a place for sharpening the mind, refining decision‑making, 
and learning to move through life with computational grace. 
*Algorithms to Live By* offers a set of mental katas—practical, elegant, and battle‑tested. 
Below is a distilled map of the territory, written in a style that fits the dojo’s ethos: 
minimal, sharp, and immediately useful.

---

## 1. Optimal Stopping — *The Art of Knowing When to Choose*
Life is full of moments where you must pick one option among many. 
The 37% Rule becomes your stance: 
observe without acting, then commit when the next opportunity surpasses all before it. 
This kata teaches timing, restraint, and decisive action.

---

## 2. Explore / Exploit — *Balancing Curiosity and Mastery*
Every choice is a duel between the new and the known. 
When time is abundant, explore. 
When time is short, exploit. 
This chapter trains you to sense your horizon and act accordingly.

---

## 3. Sorting — *Order as a Tool, Not a Religion*
Perfect organization is expensive. 
Sometimes the optimal system is controlled chaos. 
Sorting teaches you to arrange your world only as much as it serves you—and no more.

---

## 4. Caching — *Keep What You Need Close*
Your mind and environment thrive when frequently used items are within reach. 
Caching is the discipline of proximity: place what matters where it matters.

---

## 5. Scheduling — *Mastering the Queue*
Tasks compete for your attention. 
Algorithms like “shortest job first” and “earliest due date” become strategies for flow. 
This chapter is a kata in reducing friction and reclaiming time.

---

## 6. Bayes’s Rule — *Updating Your Beliefs*
Every new piece of information is a chance to refine your worldview. 
Bayes’s Rule teaches you to adjust, adapt, 
and evolve with precision rather than instinct.

---

## 7. Overfitting — *Avoiding the Trap of False Patterns*
Humans overfit too—seeing meaning where none exists.
This chapter teaches the discipline of simplicity, 
the value of noise, and the wisdom of not clinging too tightly to the past.

---

## 8. Relaxation — *Loosening Constraints to Solve the Impossible*
When a problem is too rigid, bend it. 
Relaxation algorithms show that allowing temporary rule‑breaking can reveal paths forward. 
Flexibility becomes a weapon.

---

## 9. Randomness — *The Power of the Unpredictable*
Randomness protects, simplifies, and sometimes outperforms careful planning. 
This kata teaches you when to shuffle the deck—literally and metaphorically.

---

## 10. Networking — *Understanding Systems, Not Just Nodes*
Traffic, communication, and collaboration all follow network dynamics. 
Sometimes adding more capacity makes things worse. 
This chapter trains you to see the invisible forces that shape collective behavior.

---

## 11. Game Theory — *Strategy in a World of Others*
Life is not single‑player. 
Game theory offers tools for cooperation, competition, and navigating shared resources. 
Learn the moves, anticipate the counters.

---

## 12. Computational Kindness — *Reducing Cognitive Burden for Others*
The final kata: kindness as optimization. 
Make choices easier for others. Offer defaults. 
Remove friction. True courtesy is computational.

---

## Closing Words
This book is a dojo of decision‑making. 
Each chapter is a practice. 
Each algorithm is a stance. 
Use them not as rigid rules but as tools—flexible, sharp, and ready for whatever life throws your way.
